## Chapter 2: Building AI with a Conscience: Responsible AI Principles — Practice Questions

### Multiple Choice

**Q1.** Which of the following is NOT one of Microsoft's six guiding principles for Responsible AI?

A) Fairness
B) Profitability
C) Transparency
D) Accountability

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Profitability is not one of Microsoft's six Responsible AI principles. The six principles are: Fairness, Reliability and Safety, Privacy and Security, Inclusiveness, Transparency, and Accountability. While business success matters, ethical AI development prioritizes these human-centered principles over profit.

</details>

---

**Q2.** A hiring AI system consistently ranks male candidates higher than equally qualified female candidates. Which Responsible AI principle is being violated?

A) Reliability and Safety
B) Transparency
C) Fairness
D) Inclusiveness

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C**

Fairness is being violated because the AI system is treating people differently based on gender rather than their actual qualifications. Fairness requires that AI systems treat all groups of people equitably without discrimination based on characteristics like gender, race, or age.

</details>

**Q3.** What is the primary purpose of Microsoft's Transparency Notes?

A) To explain pricing for AI services
B) To provide documentation about AI system capabilities, limitations, and intended uses
C) To list all the code used in AI models
D) To share financial reports about AI products

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Transparency Notes are documents that help users understand how Microsoft's AI technologies work, including their capabilities, limitations, and intended uses. They support the Transparency principle by giving users the information they need to make informed decisions about using AI systems.

</details>

**Q4.** An AI-powered medical diagnosis tool must perform consistently and handle unexpected situations without causing harm to patients. Which Responsible AI principle does this requirement address?

A) Privacy and Security
B) Reliability and Safety
C) Inclusiveness
D) Accountability

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Reliability and Safety ensures AI systems operate consistently and safely, especially in high-stakes scenarios like healthcare. This principle requires that AI systems handle unexpected inputs gracefully and don't cause physical or emotional harm to users.

</details>

**Q5.** Which Responsible AI principle ensures that AI systems work well for people with disabilities?

A) Fairness
B) Transparency
C) Inclusiveness
D) Privacy and Security

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C**

Inclusiveness means AI systems should be designed to engage and empower everyone, including people with disabilities. This principle ensures that AI benefits are accessible to all people regardless of their physical abilities, age, gender, or other characteristics.

</details>

### True / False

**Q6.** Accountability in Responsible AI means that AI systems should make decisions completely autonomously without any human oversight. — **True / False**

*False. Accountability actually requires the opposite—it means people should be responsible for AI systems and their outcomes. Humans must maintain oversight and control over AI decisions, especially for consequential decisions that affect people's lives. Organizations and individuals must be answerable for the AI systems they design and deploy.*

---

**Q7.** Privacy and Security in Responsible AI only concerns protecting data from hackers and external threats. — **True / False**

*False. Privacy and Security encompasses much more than just protection from external threats. It also includes giving users control over their data, being transparent about data collection and usage, ensuring data is used only for intended purposes, and respecting user consent. It addresses both how data is protected and how it is handled throughout the AI system lifecycle.*

### Short Answer

**Q8.** Explain why transparency is important in AI systems and give one example of how it can be implemented.

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

Transparency is important because it helps users understand how AI systems work, what data they use, and how they reach decisions. This understanding builds trust and allows users to make informed choices about using AI. One example of implementing transparency is providing clear explanations when an AI system denies a loan application, so the applicant understands what factors influenced the decision and can potentially address them.

</details>

### Scenario-Based

**Q10.** A retail company is developing an AI-powered customer service chatbot. During testing, they discover that the chatbot sometimes provides inaccurate product information, struggles to understand customers who speak English as a second language, and stores conversation logs without clearly informing users. Identify which three Responsible AI principles are being violated and briefly explain how each violation should be addressed.

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

Three Responsible AI principles are being violated:

1. **Reliability and Safety**: The chatbot providing inaccurate product information violates this principle. This should be addressed by improving the chatbot's training data, implementing quality checks, and adding fallback options to connect customers with human agents when the AI is uncertain.

2. **Inclusiveness**: The chatbot struggling with non-native English speakers violates this principle. This should be addressed by training the model on diverse speech patterns, adding support for multiple languages, and testing with users from various linguistic backgrounds.

3. **Privacy and Security**: Storing conversation logs without informing users violates this principle. This should be addressed by adding clear notifications about data collection, obtaining user consent, providing options to opt out, and explaining how the data will be used and protected.

</details>
