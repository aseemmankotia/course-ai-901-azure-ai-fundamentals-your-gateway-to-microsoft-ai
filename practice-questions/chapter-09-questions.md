**Q1.** What is the total time allocated for the AI-901 Azure AI Fundamentals exam?

**Q3.** When encountering a drag-and-drop question on the AI-901 exam, what is the best approach?

A) Skip it and return at the end since they take too long
B) Read all available options first, then match the most obvious pairs before tackling uncertain ones
C) Randomly place items and hope for partial credit
D) Only answer if you are 100% certain of every placement

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Reading all options first and starting with obvious matches is the most effective strategy. Option A wastes valuable time by requiring you to re-read the question later. Option C is counterproductive as guessing without strategy reduces your chances. Option D is impractical since partial credit may be available, and educated guesses on uncertain items are better than leaving blanks.

</details>

**Q4.** What is a common exam trap regarding Azure AI service names?

A) Services are always named exactly as they appear in documentation
B) Similar-sounding services like "Computer Vision" and "Custom Vision" may be confused in answer options
C) All Azure AI services have completely unique and unrelated names
D) Service names never appear in exam questions

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Microsoft often includes similar-sounding services in answer options to test precise understanding. Computer Vision (pre-built models) and Custom Vision (trainable models) serve different purposes despite similar names. Options A and C are incorrect because service naming can be confusing. Option D is wrong because service identification is a core exam topic.

</details>

### True / False

**Q6.** On the AI-901 exam, you can return to previous questions and change your answers before submitting. — **True / False**

*True. The AI-901 exam allows you to navigate back to previous questions and modify your answers before final submission. This is why flagging uncertain questions for review is a recommended strategy, as you can revisit them after completing the entire exam with any remaining time.*

---

**Q7.** Case study questions on the AI-901 exam always require you to read every detail of the scenario to answer correctly. — **True / False**

*False. While case studies provide detailed scenarios, many questions focus on specific aspects. Effective time management involves quickly identifying which parts of the scenario are relevant to each question rather than memorizing every detail. Scanning for keywords related to the specific question saves valuable time.*

**Q9.** What time management strategy should you use if you encounter a question you find extremely difficult during the exam?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

Flag the question for review, make your best educated guess, and move on to the next question. Return to flagged questions only after completing all other questions. This prevents spending excessive time on one difficult question while potentially missing easier questions later in the exam that could earn you points.

</details>

### Scenario-Based

**Q10.** Sarah is taking the AI-901 exam and has 15 minutes remaining with 8 questions left to answer. She notices that 3 of the remaining questions are case study questions with lengthy scenarios, and 5 are standard multiple choice questions. Two of the case study questions are flagged for review because she was uncertain about them earlier.

What strategy should Sarah use to maximize her score with the remaining time?

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

Sarah should first quickly answer the 5 standard multiple choice questions, as these typically require less time to read and answer. This ensures she doesn't leave easy points on the table. With any remaining time, she should return to the 3 case study questions, prioritizing the one she hasn't attempted yet over the 2 flagged questions (since she already provided answers for those). For case study questions, she should scan for keywords relevant to each specific question rather than re-reading entire scenarios. If time is critically short, she should ensure every question has an answer, even if it's an educated guess, since there is no penalty for incorrect answers.

</details>
