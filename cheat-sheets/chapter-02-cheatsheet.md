## Chapter 2: Building AI with a Conscience: Responsible AI Principles — Quick Reference

### Core Concepts
| Concept | One-line explanation |
|---------|---------------------|
| Fairness | AI systems should treat all people equitably without bias based on gender, ethnicity, or other factors |
| Reliability & Safety | AI must perform consistently and safely without causing harm to users |
| Privacy & Security | AI systems must protect personal data and prevent unauthorized access |
| Inclusiveness | AI should be accessible and beneficial to people of all abilities and backgrounds |
| Transparency | AI decisions should be understandable and explainable to users |
| Accountability | People must be responsible for AI systems and their outcomes |
| Responsible AI Standard | Microsoft's framework for implementing the six principles in practice |
| Transparency Notes | Documentation explaining AI system capabilities, limitations, and intended use |

### Key Principles Framework
```
Microsoft's 6 Responsible AI Principles:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  F - Fairness                           │
│  R - Reliability & Safety               │
│  P - Privacy & Security                 │
│  I - Inclusiveness                      │
│  T - Transparency                       │
│  A - Accountability                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
Memory aid: "FRPITA" or "Fair, Reliable, Private, Inclusive, Transparent, Accountable"
```

### Common Patterns
**Pattern 1: Impact Assessment**
Before deployment, evaluate potential harms across all six principles for affected stakeholders.

**Pattern 2: Human-in-the-Loop**
Maintain human oversight and intervention capability for high-stakes AI decisions.

### Things to Remember
✅ All six principles must be considered together—they're interconnected
✅ Transparency Notes are required documentation for Azure AI services
✅ Accountability always rests with humans, not the AI system
✅ Inclusiveness means designing for accessibility from the start
❌ Don't assume fairness—actively test for bias across demographic groups
❌ Don't deploy without understanding limitations and failure modes
