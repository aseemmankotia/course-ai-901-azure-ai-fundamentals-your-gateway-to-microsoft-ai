### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Domain Weighting | The percentage of exam questions allocated to each content area, indicating relative importance |
| Case Study Questions | Multi-part scenarios requiring analysis of a business situation and selection of appropriate AI solutions |
| Drag-and-Drop | Question format requiring correct ordering or matching of elements by dragging them into position |
| Distractor | An incorrect answer option designed to seem plausible and test deeper understanding |
| Azure AI Foundry | Microsoft's unified platform for building, deploying, and managing AI solutions (formerly Azure AI Studio) |
| Responsible AI | Microsoft's framework of six principles guiding ethical AI development and deployment |
| Pre-built Cognitive Services | Ready-to-use AI APIs requiring no machine learning expertise (Vision, Language, Speech, Decision) |

### Memory Tricks
- **"40-60 Rule"**: AI concepts are 40ish%, Foundry skills are 60ish%—remember "concepts come first but practice weighs more"
- **"FAIR-PT"** for Responsible AI: Fairness, Accountability, Inclusiveness, Reliability, Privacy, Transparency
- **"MCD" for question types**: Multiple choice, Case studies, Drag-and-drop—like ordering at a restaurant, you get Multiple options, a Case (scenario) to consider, and you Drag your answer to the plate
- **"Simple First" Rule**: When in doubt, Azure usually wants the simplest pre-built solution—don't overcomplicate!
