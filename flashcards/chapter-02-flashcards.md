## Chapter 2: Building AI with a Conscience: Responsible AI Principles — Flashcards

| # | Front (Question) | Back (Answer) |
|---|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | What are Microsoft's six Responsible AI principles? | Fairness, Reliability & Safety, Privacy & Security, Inclusiveness, Transparency, and Accountability |
| 2 | What does Fairness mean in the context of Responsible AI? | AI systems should treat all people equitably without bias based on gender, ethnicity, or other characteristics |
| 3 | Why is Reliability & Safety important in AI systems? | AI must perform consistently as intended and avoid causing harm to users or society |
| 4 | How does Privacy & Security apply to Responsible AI? | AI systems must protect personal data, comply with privacy laws, and be secure against attacks |
| 5 | What does Inclusiveness mean for AI development? | AI solutions should be designed to work for people of all abilities, backgrounds, and circumstances |
| 6 | What is Transparency in Responsible AI? | AI systems should be understandable, with clear explanations of how they work and make decisions |
| 7 | What does Accountability require in AI systems? | People must be responsible for how AI systems operate and their outcomes |
| 8 | What is Microsoft's Responsible AI Standard? | A framework of requirements and guidelines that Microsoft teams must follow when building AI products |
| 9 | What are Transparency Notes? | Documentation that helps users understand how an AI technology works, its capabilities, and limitations |
| 10 | How can AI fairness be tested? | By evaluating model performance across different demographic groups to detect disparate outcomes |
| 11 | What is an example of an AI safety concern? | An autonomous vehicle making incorrect decisions that could endanger passengers or pedestrians |
| 12 | Why might an AI system lack inclusiveness? | Training data may not represent diverse populations, or interfaces may not accommodate disabilities |
| 13 | What role do humans play in AI accountability? | Humans must oversee AI decisions, take responsibility for outcomes, and intervene when needed |
| 14 | How does transparency help build trust in AI? | Users can understand AI limitations and make informed decisions about when to rely on AI outputs |
| 15 | Is Responsible AI optional for Azure AI solutions? | No, Responsible AI principles must guide every AI solution built on Azure |

### Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Fairness | The principle that AI systems should treat all people equitably without discrimination |
| Reliability & Safety | AI systems performing consistently and safely without causing harm |
| Inclusiveness | Designing AI to engage and empower everyone regardless of ability or background |
| Transparency | Making AI systems understandable with clear explanations of functionality |
| Accountability | Ensuring people are responsible for AI system design, deployment, and outcomes |
| Transparency Notes | Microsoft documentation explaining AI capabilities, limitations, and intended uses |
| Responsible AI Standard | Microsoft's internal framework of mandatory practices for building ethical AI |

### Memory Tricks
- **FRIPTI** - Remember the six principles: **F**airness, **R**eliability, **I**nclusiveness, **P**rivacy, **T**ransparency, **A**ccountability (sounds like "Fifty")
- Think of AI like a **responsible employee**: Fair to coworkers, Reliable at work, Respects Privacy, Includes everyone, Transparent about actions, Accountable for mistakes
- **"TRUST AI"** - Transparency, Reliability, Understandable (Accountability), Security/Safety, Treatment fair (Fairness), Inclusive
